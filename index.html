Generalization of the Smagorinsky Model for Turbulent Flow Simulations

Published in Applied Mathematics and Computation

In this publication, we explore a novel approach to improve large eddy simulations (LES) of turbulent flows, specifically addressing the over-dissipation inherent in the Smagorinsky model. Through our research, we propose a generalization of the model that mitigates this common issue, improving the accuracy of simulations.

Our study includes finite element analysis and numerical computations based on benchmark problems in both 2-D and 3-D, providing a thorough investigation into the stability, convergence, and performance of the proposed model. The findings are crucial for advancing the efficiency and reliability of LES in practical applications.

  • Key Highlights:
  • Generalization of the Smagorinsky model to reduce over-dissipation.
  • Comprehensive finite element stability and convergence analysis.
  • Three numerical simulations on benchmark problems in 2-D and 3-D.

    • For further details, you can read the full article on ScienceDirect.

    Solving Boundary Value and Initial Boundary Value Problems of Partial Differential Equations Using Meshless Methods

    Abstract: In this dissertation, the methods of fundamental solutions (MFS) and the methods of particular solutions (MPS) are used to solve the boundary value problems of the Poisson and Helmholtz equations, where the particular solutions of the Poisson and Helmholtz equations are used. Then the initial boundary value problems of the diffusion and wave equations are discretized into a sequence of boundary value problems of the Helmholtz equation by using either the Laplace transform or time difference methods. The Helmholtz problems are solved consequently in an iterative manner which leads to the solution of the original diffusion or wave equation. Various numerical examples are presented in this dissertation to show the efficiencies of the purposed methods. Some further work including solving Stoke's flow problems is also purposed here.
