Published in Applied Mathematics and Computation

In this publication, we explore a novel approach to improve large eddy simulations (LES) of turbulent flows, specifically addressing the over-dissipation inherent in the Smagorinsky model. The Smagorinsky model is widely used for simulating turbulent flow, but it tends to cause excessive dissipation in simulations, especially in situations involving complex flow structures.

Through our research, we propose a generalization of the model that mitigates this common issue, improving the accuracy of simulations. We introduce an updated formulation for the subgrid-scale stresses in turbulent flows, which reduces the dissipation in the model while preserving the accuracy of the simulations.

The study presents a comprehensive stability and convergence analysis of the modified model using finite element methods. We also perform numerical simulations on several benchmark problems, both in two and three dimensions, to demonstrate the efficacy of the new model compared to the traditional Smagorinsky model.

  • Generalization of the Smagorinsky model to reduce over-dissipation.
  • Comprehensive finite element stability and convergence analysis.
  • Numerical simulations on benchmark problems in 2-D and 3-D.

For further details, you can read the full article on ScienceDirect.



Abstract: In this paper, we propose a novel meshless approach to solve the boundary value problems of diffusion and wave equations, which are common in various fields of science and engineering. The method is based on the approximate particular solutions of Helmholtz equations, which are used to solve the initial boundary value problems for time-dependent diffusion and wave equations.

The approach combines the methods of fundamental solutions (MFS) with the methods of particular solutions (MPS). The initial boundary value problems are discretized into a sequence of Helmholtz equations, which can then be solved iteratively using either the Laplace transform or time difference methods.

Several numerical examples are presented to show the efficiency and accuracy of the proposed method in solving both diffusion and wave equations. These examples include applications in heat conduction, wave propagation, and other physical phenomena that can be modeled by partial differential equations.

  • Introduction of a meshless approach for solving diffusion and wave equations.
  • Use of Helmholtz equations and combination of MFS and MPS methods.
  • Detailed numerical examples demonstrating the method's efficiency.

For more details, you can access the full article on arXiv.



Abstract: This dissertation focuses on the application of meshless methods for solving boundary value problems (BVPs) and initial boundary value problems (IBVPs) for partial differential equations (PDEs), which are commonly encountered in engineering and physics. The methods of fundamental solutions (MFS) and particular solutions (MPS) are employed to solve the boundary value problems of Poisson and Helmholtz equations.

To address time-dependent PDEs, the initial boundary value problems (IBVPs) for diffusion and wave equations are discretized into a sequence of boundary value problems of Helmholtz equations. The problem-solving procedure involves either using the Laplace transform or time difference methods, followed by an iterative solution of the resulting Helmholtz problems. These methods provide a computationally efficient way to obtain solutions to complex PDEs.

Several numerical examples are included in the dissertation to demonstrate the effectiveness and efficiency of the proposed meshless methods in various applications, such as fluid dynamics and heat transfer.

  • Use of MFS and MPS to solve BVPs and IBVPs of Poisson and Helmholtz equations.
  • Application of meshless methods to time-dependent diffusion and wave equations.
  • Numerical examples illustrating the efficiency of the methods.
